⚛️ Quantum Cryptography & Post-Quantum Security

Quantum Computing vs Classical Cryptography

Quantum cryptography leverages quantum mechanical properties like superposition and entanglement to create unbreakable communication channels. However, quantum computers also threaten classical cryptographic systems.

Post-quantum cryptography develops new mathematical foundations resistant to both classical and quantum attacks, preparing for the quantum computing era.

⚛️ Quantum Mathematical Foundations

Qubit State: |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ where |α|² + |β|² = 1

No-Cloning Theorem: ∀|ψ⟩: ∄U such that U|ψ⟩|0⟩ = |ψ⟩|ψ⟩

Quantum Entanglement: |ψ⟩ = (1/√2)(|00⟩ + |11⟩)

Uncertainty Principle: ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2
